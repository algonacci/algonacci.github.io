# AI CV Positioning Strategy

## Professional Title
AI Systems Engineer

## Alternate Titles
- Applied AI Engineer
- Generative AI Engineer
- ML Engineer
- AI Engineer

## Professional Summary
AI-focused engineer with a systems-oriented background spanning machine learning, generative AI, computer vision, forecasting, and cloud-native delivery. Combines model-level understanding with practical infrastructure experience across Docker, Linux, observability, virtualization, and deployment workflows. Best positioned for roles that need someone who can move between experimentation, evaluation, application engineering, and production execution.

## Key Differentiators
- Bridges AI application work and infrastructure execution.
- Strong fit for AI systems roles, not only model training roles.
- Has public technical output: projects, deployment guides, benchmarking content, and speaking experience.
- Can be positioned as both engineering-oriented and research-literate.
- Brings Indonesian-language dataset and local-market context that is useful for applied NLP work.

## Strengths To Emphasize
- LLM systems and GenAI evaluation
- AI product implementation, not only coursework
- Cloud-native deployment and containerization
- Computer vision and applied ML breadth
- Research communication and technical mentoring
- Infrastructure fluency that supports production AI delivery

## ATS Keywords
AI Systems Engineer, Applied AI, Machine Learning, Generative AI, LLM, RAG, Prompt Engineering, Embeddings, Model Evaluation, AI Infrastructure, MLOps, Computer Vision, Forecasting, Docker, Cloud Run, Linux, Observability, Vector Database, Retrieval Systems, Cloud Deployment, NLP, AI Benchmarking, PyTorch, OpenCV, FastAPI, Flask

## Emphasize
- Systems thinking and production orientation
- End-to-end ownership from experiment to deployment
- Benchmarking and evaluation literacy
- AI engineering breadth across NLP, CV, forecasting, and cloud
- Public technical communication and mentoring

## Reduce Or Remove
- Student framing
- Generic soft-skills lists
- Unrelated hospitality wording beyond the education line
- Long descriptions without architecture, tooling, or technical outcomes
- Placeholder/template content
